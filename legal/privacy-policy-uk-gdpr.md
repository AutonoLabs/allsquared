## 4. LAWFUL BASES FOR PROCESSING

Under UK GDPR, we must have a lawful basis for each processing activity. We rely on the following bases:

### 4.1 Contract Performance (Article 6(1)(b))

We process personal data where it is necessary for the performance of our contract with you (i.e., our Terms of Service) or to take steps at your request before entering into a contract. This includes:

- Creating and managing your Account;
- Facilitating engagements between Clients and Freelancers;
- Processing payments and managing Escrow;
- Providing the Platform's core features (messaging, project management, dispute resolution);
- Verifying your identity for Account security;
- Generating invoices and fee statements;
- Communicating with you about your Account and Projects; and
- Enforcing our Terms of Service.

### 4.2 Legitimate Interests (Article 6(1)(f))

We process personal data where it is necessary for our legitimate interests (or those of a third party), provided your interests and fundamental rights do not override those interests. We have conducted legitimate interest assessments for each of the following:

| Purpose | Legitimate Interest | Balancing Factors |
|---------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Platform security and fraud prevention | Protecting our Users and Platform from fraud, abuse, and security threats | Essential for all Users' safety; limited to necessary data; automated decisions subject to human review |
| Service improvement and analytics | Understanding how the Platform is used to improve features and user experience | Based on anonymised/aggregated data where possible; minimal privacy impact |
| Customer support | Responding to enquiries and resolving issues | Directly expected by Users; limited to support context |
| Legal compliance and dispute resolution | Maintaining records for legal claims and regulatory compliance | Required by law or necessary for legal defence; retention periods proportionate |
| Platform integrity | Detecting and preventing Terms of Service violations, including anti-circumvention | Protects the marketplace ecosystem; proportionate to the risk |
| Network and information security | Protecting the Platform's infrastructure and data | Essential for data protection compliance |

**Your right to object:** You have the right to object to processing based on legitimate interests. See Section 8 for details.

### 4.3 Consent (Article 6(1)(a))

We process certain personal data based on your explicit, freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous consent. This includes:

- **Marketing communications**: Sending you newsletters, promotional emails, product updates, and partner offers;
- **Non-essential cookies**: Setting analytics and preference cookies (see Section 10); and
- **Optional profiling**: Using your data to personalise recommendations (e.g., suggested Freelancers or Projects).

**Your right to withdraw consent:** You may withdraw consent at any time by updating your preferences in Account settings, clicking "unsubscribe" in marketing emails, or contacting us at [privacy@allsquared.io](mailto:privacy@allsquared.io). Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.

### 4.4 Legal Obligation (Article 6(1)(c))

We process personal data where necessary to comply with a legal obligation, including:

- Tax reporting obligations to HMRC;
- Anti-money laundering (AML) and know-your-customer (KYC) requirements;
- Responding to lawful requests from law enforcement or regulatory authorities;
- Maintaining records required by the Companies Act 2006; and
- Data subject access requests and other rights requests under UK GDPR.

## 7. DATA RETENTION

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by Applicable Law. Our retention periods are:

### 7.1 Retention Schedule

| Data Category | Retention Period | Legal Basis / Rationale |
|--------------|-----------------|------------------------|
| **Account data** | Duration of Account + **7 years** post-closure | Tax and accounting records (HMRC requirements); Limitation Act 1980 (6-year limitation period + 1 year buffer) |
| **Payment and financial records** | **7 years** from date of transaction | HMRC requirements (s.12B Taxes Management Act 1970; VAT Regulations 1995, reg. 31); Companies Act 2006 (s.388) |
| **Contract and project documents** | **6 years** from project completion | Limitation Act 1980 (s.5 — 6-year limitation for breach of contract); potential claims period |
| **Communications data** | **6 years** from last message | Limitation Act 1980; evidence preservation for potential disputes |
| **Dispute records** | **6 years** from resolution | Limitation Act 1980; evidence preservation |
| **Identity verification documents** | **5 years** from verification date | Anti-money laundering regulations (Money Laundering, Terrorist Financing and Transfer of Funds (Information on the Payer) Regulations 2017, reg. 40) |
| **Usage and analytics data** | **2 years** from collection, then anonymised | Legitimate interest in service improvement; anonymised after retention period |
| **Marketing consent records** | Duration of consent + **3 years** | ICO guidance on demonstrating consent |
| **Cookie data** | See cookie-specific retention periods in Section 10 | Consent (for non-essential cookies) |
| **Server and security logs** | **12 months** | Network and information security |

### 7.2 Retention Principles

(a) We apply the principle of **data minimisation** — we do not retain data longer than necessary.

(b) At the end of a retention period, data is either **securely deleted** or **irreversibly anonymised** (so it can no longer identify any individual).

(c) Where data is subject to an ongoing legal claim, regulatory investigation, or dispute, we may extend the retention period until the matter is resolved.

(d) You may request earlier deletion of your data in certain circumstances (see Section 8.3 — Right to Erasure).

## 8. YOUR RIGHTS UNDER UK GDPR

You have the following rights in respect of your personal data. These rights are not absolute and may be subject to exemptions under Data Protection Law.

### 8.1 Right of Access (Article 15)

You have the right to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you, together with information about how we process it (a "**Subject Access Request**" or "**SAR**").

- **How to exercise:** Email [privacy@allsquared.io](mailto:privacy@allsquared.io) or use the data export feature in Account settings.
- **Response time:** Within **one calendar month** of receiving a valid request (extendable by two months for complex requests).
- **Format:** We will provide data in a commonly used, machine-readable electronic format (e.g., JSON or CSV).
- **Fee:** Free of charge, unless the request is manifestly unfounded or excessive, in which case we may charge a reasonable fee or refuse the request.

### 8.2 Right to Rectification (Article 16)

You have the right to request correction of inaccurate personal data and completion of incomplete personal data.

- **How to exercise:** Update your profile directly in Account settings, or email [privacy@allsquared.io](mailto:privacy@allsquared.io).
- **Response time:** Without undue delay, and within **one calendar month**.

### 8.3 Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten") (Article 17)

You have the right to request deletion of your personal data in certain circumstances, including where:

(a) the data is no longer necessary for the purpose for which it was collected;

(b) you withdraw consent (where consent is the lawful basis);

(c) you object to processing based on legitimate interests and there are no overriding grounds;

(d) the data has been unlawfully processed; or

(e) deletion is required to comply with a legal obligation.

**Exceptions:** We may retain data where necessary for:

- Compliance with legal obligations (e.g., HMRC tax record requirements);
- Establishment, exercise, or defence of legal claims;
- Public interest (e.g., fraud prevention records);
- Archiving in the public interest or scientific/historical research.

### 8.4 Right to Data Portability (Article 20)

You have the right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format, and to transmit it to another controller, where:

(a) the processing is based on consent or contract performance; and

(b) the processing is carried out by automated means.

- **How to exercise:** Use the data export feature in Account settings or email [privacy@allsquared.io](mailto:privacy@allsquared.io).
- **Format:** JSON or CSV.

### 8.5 Right to Restriction of Processing (Article 18)

You have the right to request that we restrict (i.e., limit) the processing of your personal data in certain circumstances, including where:

(a) you contest the accuracy of the data (restriction applies while we verify);

(b) the processing is unlawful but you do not want deletion;

(c) we no longer need the data but you need it for legal claims; or

(d) you have objected to processing (restriction applies pending verification).

### 8.6 Right to Object (Article 21)

You have the right to object to processing of your personal data based on **legitimate interests** (Section 4.2). Upon objection, we will stop processing unless we demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds that override your interests, rights, and freedoms.

You have an **absolute right to object** to processing for **direct marketing** purposes at any time.

### 8.7 Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making (Article 22)

(a) AllSquared does not currently make decisions based solely on automated processing (including profiling) that produce legal effects concerning you or similarly significantly affect you.

(b) If we introduce automated decision-making in the future, we will:

   (i) notify you and update this Policy;

   (ii) ensure a lawful basis (explicit consent, contract necessity, or authorisation by law);

   (iii) provide meaningful information about the logic involved; and

   (iv) provide a mechanism for you to request human review of any automated decision.

### 8.8 How to Exercise Your Rights

- **Email:** [privacy@allsquared.io](mailto:privacy@allsquared.io)
- **Account settings:** Use the self-service data tools in your Account
- **Post:** Data Protection Officer, AllSquared Ltd, [REGISTERED_ADDRESS]

We may ask you to verify your identity before processing your request. We will respond to all valid requests within **one calendar month** (extendable by two months for complex or numerous requests, with notification).

## 9. DATA SECURITY

### 9.1 Technical Measures

We implement appropriate technical measures to protect your personal data, including:

(a) **Encryption**: All data is encrypted in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest (AES-256);

(b) **Authentication**: Multi-factor authentication available for all Accounts; session management via Clerk;

(c) **Access controls**: Role-based access controls; principle of least privilege; access logging;

(d) **Infrastructure security**: Hosted on Vercel with enterprise-grade security; database hosted by Neon with automated backups and point-in-time recovery;

(e) **Monitoring**: Continuous security monitoring, intrusion detection, and anomaly alerting;

(f) **Vulnerability management**: Regular security assessments and dependency scanning; and

(g) **Incident response**: Documented incident response plan with defined escalation procedures.

### 9.2 Organisational Measures

(a) Data protection training for all staff with access to personal data;

(b) Confidentiality obligations for all employees and contractors;

(c) Data Processing Agreements with all third-party processors;

(d) Regular review and updating of security policies and procedures; and

(e) Data protection impact assessments (DPIAs) for high-risk processing activities.

### 9.3 Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach:

(a) We will notify the **ICO within 72 hours** of becoming aware of a breach that is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms (as required by UK GDPR Article 33);

(b) We will notify **affected individuals without undue delay** where the breach is likely to result in a high risk to their rights and freedoms (as required by UK GDPR Article 34); and

(c) We will maintain an internal breach register documenting all breaches, their effects, and the remedial actions taken.

## 10. COOKIES

### 10.1 What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit the Platform. We use cookies and similar technologies (e.g., local storage, session storage) for the purposes described below.

### 10.2 Types of Cookies We Use

#### Essential Cookies (No Consent Required)

These cookies are strictly necessary for the Platform to function. They cannot be disabled without affecting Platform functionality.

| Cookie | Provider | Purpose | Duration |
|--------|----------|---------|----------|
| `__clerk_session` | Clerk | User authentication session management | Session (expires on logout or after inactivity) |
| `__clerk_db_jwt` | Clerk | Secure authentication token | Session |
| `csrf_token` | AllSquared | Cross-site request forgery protection | Session |
| `cookie_consent` | AllSquared | Stores your cookie preferences | 12 months |

**Lawful basis:** These cookies are necessary for the performance of our contract with you and/or our legitimate interest in Platform security. Consent is not required under the Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations 2003 (PECR), Regulation 6.

#### Analytics Cookies (Consent Required — Opt-In)

These cookies help us understand how Users interact with the Platform so we can improve it. **They are only set with your explicit consent.**

| Cookie | Provider | Purpose | Duration |
|--------|----------|---------|----------|
| Analytics cookies | AllSquared / self-hosted analytics | Page views, feature usage, performance monitoring | 24 months |

**Lawful basis:** Consent (UK GDPR Article 6(1)(a); PECR Regulation 6).

**Note:** We do not use Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, or any third-party advertising or tracking cookies.

#### No Third-Party Advertising Cookies

AllSquared does **not** use third-party advertising cookies, tracking pixels, or retargeting technologies. We do not serve targeted advertising on the Platform and do not share your data with ad networks.

### 10.3 Managing Cookies

You can manage your cookie preferences at any time:

(a) **Cookie banner:** When you first visit the Platform, you will see a cookie consent banner allowing you to accept or reject non-essential cookies;

(b) **Account settings:** You can update your cookie preferences in your Account settings at any time;

(c) **Browser settings:** You can configure your browser to block or delete cookies. Note that blocking essential cookies may prevent the Platform from functioning correctly.

### 10.4 Do Not Track

The Platform currently does not respond to "Do Not Track" (DNT) browser signals, as there is no industry-standard protocol for DNT. However, our cookie practices provide you with direct control over non-essential cookies.
