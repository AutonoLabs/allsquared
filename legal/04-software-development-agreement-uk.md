## Background

(a) The Client wishes to engage the Developer to design, develop, test, and deliver the software project known as "{{projectName}}" on the terms set out in this Agreement.

## 1. Definitions and Interpretation

1.1 In this Agreement, unless the context requires otherwise, the following terms have the meanings set out below:

(a) "Acceptance Criteria" means the criteria against which each Deliverable or Milestone is tested to determine whether it conforms to the Specifications, as set out in Schedule 1 or the relevant Milestone description in Schedule 2.

(b) "Acceptance Tests" means the tests carried out by the Client (or its nominee) to determine whether the Software or a Deliverable meets the Acceptance Criteria.

(c) "Background IP" means any IP Rights owned by or licensed to a Party before the Effective Date or developed independently of this Agreement.

(d) "Bug" means any error, flaw, or deficiency in the Software that causes it to produce incorrect or unexpected results or to behave in unintended ways. Bugs are classified as:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(i) "Critical Bug" — the Software is inoperable or a core function is unavailable with no workaround;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(ii) "Major Bug" — a significant function does not operate as specified but a workaround exists; and

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(iii) "Minor Bug" — a cosmetic issue or minor functional issue that does not materially affect operation.

(e) "Change Request" means a written request by either Party to amend the Specifications, scope, Deliverables, timeline, or fees under this Agreement.

(f) "Defect" means a failure of the Software to conform materially to the Specifications.

(g) "Deliverables" means all items to be delivered by the Developer under this Agreement, including the Software, Source Code, Documentation, and any other materials specified in Schedule 1 or Schedule 2.

(h) "Documentation" means all user manuals, technical documentation, API references, installation guides, and other written materials relating to the Software to be provided by the Developer under this Agreement.

(i) "Foreground IP" means all IP Rights created by or on behalf of the Developer in the performance of this Agreement, including in the Software, Source Code, Documentation, and all Deliverables.

(j) "IP Rights" means all intellectual property rights of any kind, including patents, trade marks, rights in designs, copyright, database rights, rights in know-how, trade secrets, and all other intellectual property rights, in each case whether registered or unregistered and including all applications and rights to apply for and be granted renewals or extensions of, and rights to claim priority from, such rights and all similar or equivalent rights or forms of protection in any part of the world.

(k) "Repository" means the version control repository (such as Git) used by the Developer to store and manage the Source Code, to which the Client must be granted access in accordance with clause 3.3.

(l) "Software" means the software application, system, or code to be developed by the Developer as described in Schedule 1 (Specifications).

(m) "Source Code" means the human-readable programming code of the Software, including all scripts, configuration files, build files, and associated comments and documentation embedded in the code.

(n) "Specifications" means the detailed functional, technical, and design specifications for the Software set out in Schedule 1.

(o) "Working Day" means any day other than a Saturday, Sunday, or public holiday in England and Wales.

1.2 In this Agreement:

(a) a reference to a statute or statutory provision includes any subordinate legislation made under it and any modification, re-enactment, or replacement of it from time to time;

(b) a reference to writing or written includes email;

(c) the words "include", "including", and "in particular" do not limit the generality of any preceding words; and

(d) headings are for convenience only and do not affect interpretation.

## 2. Project Scope

2.1 The Developer must design, develop, test, and deliver the Software in accordance with the Specifications set out in Schedule 1.

2.2 The scope of the project is limited to what is described in Schedule 1. Any work beyond that scope requires a Change Request.

2.3 Either Party may submit a Change Request in writing. Each Change Request must describe the proposed change, its impact on the Specifications, timeline, and fees, and any other relevant details. No Change Request is effective unless agreed in writing by both Parties. Change Requests must be priced and scheduled separately from the original Milestones.

## 3. Development Process

3.1 The Developer must carry out the development work on a milestone basis in accordance with Schedule 2 (Milestone and Payment Schedule). The project start date is {{projectStartDate}} and the estimated completion date is {{estimatedCompletionDate}}.

3.2 The Developer must provide the Client with written status updates at least once per week, summarising progress against the current Milestone, any risks or issues identified, and anticipated next steps.

3.3 The Developer must grant the Client read access to the Repository throughout the term of this Agreement. The Developer must commit code to the Repository regularly and must not maintain a separate version of the Source Code outside the Repository.

3.4 The Developer must follow reasonable coding standards and best practices, including appropriate code commenting, version control discipline, and adherence to any technical standards specified in Schedule 1.

## 4. Payment

4.1 The total fee for the project is {{totalFee}} (exclusive of VAT), payable in accordance with the payment schedule set out at {{paymentSchedule}} and detailed in Schedule 2.

4.2 All payments must be processed through AllSquared's escrow facility ("Escrow"). The Client must deposit the payment for each Milestone into Escrow before the Developer commences work on that Milestone.

4.3 Escrow funds for a Milestone are released to the Developer only upon the Client's Acceptance of that Milestone in accordance with clause 6.

4.4 No payment is due for any Milestone that has not achieved Acceptance.

4.5 Fees for Change Requests must be agreed in writing as part of the Change Request process and are payable separately via Escrow upon Acceptance of the work described in the Change Request.

4.6 All fees are exclusive of VAT. If VAT is chargeable, the Developer must issue a valid VAT invoice, and the Client must pay the VAT amount in addition to the fees.

4.7 The Developer is solely responsible for all income tax, National Insurance contributions, and any other taxes arising from the fees received under this Agreement.

## 5. Intellectual Property

5.1 All Foreground IP, including all rights in the Source Code, Software, Documentation, and Deliverables, vests in the Developer upon creation and is hereby assigned to the Client with full title guarantee, effective upon receipt of full payment of the total fee. The Developer must execute all documents and do all acts reasonably required to perfect the assignment.

5.2 The Developer retains ownership of all Background IP. To the extent that any Background IP is embedded in or necessary for the use of the Deliverables, the Developer grants the Client a non-exclusive, perpetual, irrevocable, royalty-free, worldwide licence to use, modify, reproduce, and sublicence that Background IP solely as part of or in connection with the Deliverables.

5.3 The Developer must disclose in Schedule 3 all free and open source software ("FOSS") components incorporated into or bundled with the Software, including the component name, licence type, version, and manner of use. The Developer must not incorporate any FOSS component that would impose obligations on the Client to disclose or release the Source Code (such as under a copyleft licence) without the Client's prior written consent.

5.4 To the fullest extent permitted by law, the Developer irrevocably and unconditionally waives all moral rights under Chapter IV of Part I of the Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988 in respect of the Deliverables.

## 6. Acceptance Testing

6.1 Upon delivery of each Milestone Deliverable, the Client has {{acceptancePeriodDays}} Working Days to carry out Acceptance Tests against the Acceptance Criteria.

6.2 If the Deliverable passes the Acceptance Tests, the Client must issue written notice of Acceptance, and the relevant Escrow funds are released to the Developer.

6.3 If the Deliverable fails the Acceptance Tests, the Client must provide written notice specifying the failures in reasonable detail. The Developer must remedy the identified failures within 14 days and resubmit the Deliverable for testing.

6.4 The Developer is entitled to a maximum of 2 resubmissions per Milestone. If the Deliverable still fails after the second resubmission, the Client may:

(a) grant a further remediation period at the Client's discretion;

(b) accept the Deliverable with agreed deductions from the Milestone payment; or

(c) terminate this Agreement for cause in accordance with clause 13.1.

6.5 If the Client does not provide written notice of acceptance or rejection within {{acceptancePeriodDays}} Working Days of delivery (or resubmission), the Deliverable is deemed accepted.

## 7. Warranties

7.1 The Developer warrants and represents that:

(a) the Software will materially conform to the Specifications for a period of {{warrantyPeriodMonths}} months from the date of final Acceptance ("Warranty Period");

(b) the Software will be free from material Defects during the Warranty Period;

(c) the Deliverables will not infringe the IP Rights of any third party;

(d) the Software will not contain any viruses, malware, backdoors, or other malicious code; and

(e) the Developer has full authority and capacity to enter into this Agreement and to grant the rights set out in clause 5.

7.2 During the Warranty Period, the Developer must rectify any Defect reported by the Client at no additional cost, subject to the defect rectification timelines in clause 8.

## 8. Defect Rectification

8.1 During the Warranty Period, the Developer must respond to and rectify Defects within the following timelines:

(a) **Critical Bugs:** the Developer must acknowledge the Bug within 4 hours and deliver a fix or workaround within 48 hours of the Bug being reported;

(b) **Major Bugs:** the Developer must acknowledge the Bug within 1 Working Day and deliver a fix within 5 Working Days of the Bug being reported; and

(c) **Minor Bugs:** the Developer must include the fix in the next scheduled release or update, or within 20 Working Days, whichever is sooner.

8.2 The Developer must keep the Client informed of progress on all reported Bugs and must provide updates at least every 24 hours for Critical Bugs and every 3 Working Days for Major Bugs.

## 9. Support and Maintenance

9.1 For a period of {{supportTermMonths}} months following the expiry of the Warranty Period ("Support Term"), the Developer must provide support and maintenance services for the Software in accordance with Schedule 4 (Support Terms).

9.2 The scope, service levels, response times, and exclusions for support and maintenance are set out in Schedule 4.

9.3 Support and maintenance services during the Support Term are included in the total fee unless Schedule 4 specifies otherwise.

## 10. Source Code Escrow

10.1 The Developer must deposit a complete and current copy of the Source Code, together with all build scripts, configuration files, and Documentation necessary to compile, build, and deploy the Software, into a secure repository or escrow arrangement agreed by the Parties, upon completion of each Milestone.

10.2 The Client is entitled to access and use the deposited Source Code in the following circumstances:

(a) the Developer becomes insolvent, enters administration or liquidation, or makes any arrangement with its creditors;

(b) the Developer commits a material breach of this Agreement that is not remedied within the cure period specified in clause 13.1; or

(c) the Developer ceases to provide support and maintenance services at the end of the Support Term or otherwise ceases to maintain the Software.

10.3 Upon any of the events in clause 10.2, the Client is entitled to full, unrestricted access to the Source Code for the purposes of maintaining, modifying, and operating the Software.

## 11. Confidentiality

11.1 Each Party ("Receiving Party") must keep confidential all Confidential Information received from the other Party ("Disclosing Party") and must not disclose it to any third party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party.

11.2 "Confidential Information" means all information (whether disclosed in writing, orally, electronically, or by any other means) that is designated as confidential or that a reasonable person would consider confidential, including business plans, financial data, the Specifications, Source Code, technical architecture, client data, business logic, pricing, and the terms of this Agreement.

11.3 The obligations in clause 11.1 do not apply to information that:

(a) is or becomes publicly available other than through a breach of this Agreement;

(b) was already known to the Receiving Party before disclosure, as evidenced by written records;

(c) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without reference to the Confidential Information; or

(d) is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or order of a court or competent authority, provided the Receiving Party gives prompt written notice (to the extent legally permitted) and cooperates with steps to limit disclosure.

11.4 The obligations under this clause 11 survive termination or expiry of this Agreement for a period of 5 years.

## 12. Data Protection

12.1 If the Software processes personal data (as defined in the UK General Data Protection Regulation and the Data Protection Act 2018) ("UK GDPR"), the Parties must execute a data processing agreement ("DPA") that complies with Article 28 of the UK GDPR.

12.2 Where a DPA is required, the Developer acts as a data processor on behalf of the Client (as data controller) and must:

(a) process personal data only on the Client's documented instructions;

(b) ensure that persons authorised to process the personal data are subject to appropriate confidentiality obligations;

(c) implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk;

(d) assist the Client with data subject requests and other obligations under the UK GDPR; and

(e) delete or return all personal data to the Client on termination of this Agreement, at the Client's election.

12.3 The Developer must not transfer personal data outside the United Kingdom without the Client's prior written consent and without ensuring that adequate safeguards are in place in accordance with the UK GDPR.

## 13. Termination

13.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement for cause by giving written notice if:

(a) the other Party commits a material breach and fails to remedy that breach within 14 days of receiving written notice specifying the breach; or

(b) the other Party becomes insolvent, enters administration or liquidation, or makes any arrangement with its creditors.

13.2 The Client may terminate this Agreement for convenience by giving at least 30 days' written notice. Upon termination for convenience:

(a) the Client must pay for all Milestones that have been completed and accepted up to the date of termination;

(b) Escrow funds for accepted Milestones must be released to the Developer; and

(c) all Foreground IP in respect of completed and paid Milestones is assigned to the Client in accordance with clause 5.1.

13.3 The Developer may terminate this Agreement for convenience by giving at least 30 days' written notice, provided that the Developer must complete any Milestone that is substantially in progress at the date of notice and must deliver all work product completed to that date.

13.4 Upon termination or expiry for any reason:

(a) the Developer must deliver all Source Code, Documentation, and Deliverables (including partially completed work) to the Client;

(b) each Party must return or destroy the other Party's Confidential Information; and

(c) clauses 5, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12, 14, and 16 survive termination.

## 14. Limitation of Liability

14.1 Nothing in this Agreement limits or excludes either Party's liability for:

(a) death or personal injury caused by its negligence;

(b) fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation; or

(c) any other liability that cannot be limited or excluded by applicable law.

14.2 Subject to clause 14.1, neither Party is liable to the other for any indirect, consequential, special, or incidental loss or damage, including loss of profit, loss of revenue, loss of anticipated savings, loss of business, loss of data, or loss of goodwill.

14.3 Subject to clause 14.1, the Developer's total aggregate liability under or in connection with this Agreement must not exceed the total fees paid or payable under this Agreement.

14.4 Subject to clause 14.1, the Client's total aggregate liability under or in connection with this Agreement must not exceed the total fees payable by the Client under this Agreement.

## 15. Dispute Resolution

15.1 The Parties must attempt to resolve any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement through good-faith negotiation within 30 days of written notice of the dispute.

15.2 If the dispute relates to a technical matter (including whether the Software conforms to the Specifications or whether a Defect exists), either Party may refer the dispute to a technical expert jointly appointed by the Parties. If the Parties cannot agree on an expert within 10 Working Days, either Party may request the President of the British Computer Society to nominate an expert. The expert's determination is final and binding on both Parties, except in the case of manifest error.

15.3 If the dispute is not resolved under clause 15.1 or 15.2, either Party may refer the dispute to arbitration under the LCIA Rules. The arbitration must be conducted in London by a single arbitrator appointed in accordance with the LCIA Rules. The language of the arbitration must be English.

15.4 Nothing in this clause 15 prevents either Party from applying to the courts of England and Wales for interim or injunctive relief.

## 16. General

16.1 **Entire agreement.** This Agreement (including all Schedules) constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties in relation to its subject matter and supersedes all previous agreements, understandings, and representations.

16.2 **Variation.** No variation of this Agreement is effective unless it is in writing and signed by both Parties.

16.3 **Severability.** If any provision is held invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, it must be deemed modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid. If modification is not possible, the provision must be deemed deleted without affecting the remaining provisions.

16.4 **Waiver.** A waiver of any right is only effective if in writing and is not to be taken as a waiver of any subsequent right.

16.5 **Assignment.** Neither Party may assign or transfer its rights or obligations under this Agreement without the other Party's prior written consent, except that the Client may assign to a successor in title to all or substantially all of its business.

16.6 **Notices.** Any notice must be in writing and delivered by email to the addresses specified in this Agreement. A notice is deemed received on the Working Day after it is sent.

16.7 **Force majeure.** Neither Party is liable for any failure or delay resulting from circumstances beyond its reasonable control. The affected Party must notify the other promptly and use reasonable endeavours to mitigate the effect. If the event continues for more than 60 days, either Party may terminate by written notice.

16.8 **Third-party rights.** No person other than the Parties has any rights under the Contracts (Rights of Third Parties) Act 1999 to enforce any term of this Agreement.

17.1 This Agreement is governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of England and Wales.

17.2 Subject to clause 15, the courts of England and Wales have exclusive jurisdiction to settle any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement.

## Schedule 4 — Support Terms

### 1. Scope of Support

During the Support Term, the Developer must provide:

(a) diagnosis and rectification of Bugs reported by the Client;

(b) reasonable assistance with configuration and operational queries;

(c) minor updates and patches necessary to maintain the Software's conformance with the Specifications; and

(d) compatibility updates required due to changes in the Client's operating environment, to the extent reasonably practicable.

### 2. Severity Levels and Response Times

| Severity | Definition | Initial Response | Target Resolution |
|----------|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Critical | Software inoperable, core function unavailable, no workaround | 4 hours | 48 hours |
| Major | Significant function impaired, workaround available | 1 Working Day | 5 Working Days |
| Minor | Cosmetic or minor functional issue, does not materially affect operation | 3 Working Days | Next scheduled release |

### 3. Support Hours

Support is available during the Developer's standard working hours: 09:00 to 17:30 (UK time) on Working Days. Critical Bug reports received outside these hours are acknowledged by 10:00 on the next Working Day.

### 4. Exclusions

The Developer is not responsible for issues arising from:

(a) modifications to the Software made by the Client or a third party without the Developer's approval;

(b) use of the Software in a manner not contemplated by the Specifications;

(c) third-party software, hardware, or infrastructure failures outside the Developer's control;

(d) failure by the Client to implement updates or patches recommended by the Developer; or

(e) force majeure events as defined in clause 16.7.

### 5. Support Request Procedure

The Client must submit all support requests through the AllSquared Platform or by email to the Developer's support email address. Each request must include a description of the issue, the severity classification, steps to reproduce (if applicable), and any relevant screenshots or logs.
