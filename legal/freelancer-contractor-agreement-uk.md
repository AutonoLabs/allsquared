## 2. ENGAGEMENT

### 2.1 Independent Contractor

(a) The Client engages the Contractor, and the Contractor agrees, to provide the Services for the Term on the terms of this Agreement.

(b) **The Contractor is engaged as an independent contractor and not as an employee, worker, or agent of the Client.** Nothing in this Agreement shall create a relationship of employment, partnership, joint venture, or agency between the Parties.

(c) The Contractor shall not hold himself/herself out as an employee, partner, or agent of the Client.

### 2.2 Term

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue until the End Date, unless terminated earlier in accordance with clause 12.

### 2.3 No Mutuality of Obligation

(a) The Client is under no obligation to offer work to the Contractor beyond the Services described in Schedule 1.

(b) The Contractor is under no obligation to accept any further work offered by the Client.

(c) There is no mutuality of obligation between the Parties outside the terms of any specific engagement under this Agreement.

## 3. SERVICES

### 3.1 Performance

The Contractor shall:

(a) provide the Services with reasonable care, skill, and diligence;

(b) comply with the Specification;

(c) meet the Milestones and timelines set out in Schedule 1;

(d) comply with all Applicable Law; and

(e) comply with the Client's reasonable health and safety policies and rules when working at the Client's premises.

### 3.2 Control and Autonomy

(a) The Contractor shall have control over the manner and means by which the Services are performed, provided the result meets the Specification and Acceptance Criteria.

(b) The Client may specify **what** is to be delivered but shall not direct **how** the Contractor carries out the work.

(c) The Contractor shall be free to determine his/her own working hours, subject to any agreed Milestones and deadlines.

(d) The Contractor shall be free to work from any location, unless the nature of the Services requires attendance at a specified location.

### 3.3 Right of Substitution

(a) The Contractor may provide a substitute to perform the Services (or any part thereof), provided that:

   (i) the substitute possesses the necessary qualifications, skills, and experience;

   (ii) the Client has approved the substitute in writing (such approval not to be unreasonably withheld or delayed);

   (iii) the substitute enters into a confidentiality undertaking on terms no less onerous than clause 9; and

   (iv) the Contractor remains liable for the acts and omissions of the substitute as if they were the Contractor's own.

(b) The Contractor shall be responsible for the remuneration and all tax and national insurance obligations of any substitute.

### 3.4 Equipment

(a) The Contractor shall provide his/her own equipment, tools, and materials necessary for the performance of the Services, unless otherwise agreed in Schedule 1.

(b) Where the Client provides equipment or access to systems, the Contractor shall use such equipment solely for the purposes of the Services and return it upon completion or termination.

### 3.5 Other Engagements

The Contractor is free to provide services to other clients, provided such engagements do not:

(a) conflict with or impair the Contractor's ability to perform the Services;

(b) give rise to a conflict of interest; or

(c) breach any confidentiality obligations under this Agreement.

## 5. TAX AND IR35 STATUS

### 5.1 Tax Status

(a) The Contractor is responsible for all income tax, national insurance contributions, and VAT arising from the Fees paid under this Agreement.

(b) The Client shall make no deductions from the Fees for income tax or national insurance unless required by law.

(c) The Contractor shall maintain registration with HMRC as a self-employed individual or limited company, as applicable.

### 5.2 IR35 Status Determination

(a) **For medium and large clients**: The Client shall make a Status Determination Statement ("**SDS**") in accordance with Chapter 10 of Part 2 of ITEPA 2003 and provide it to the Contractor and any fee-paying intermediary before the start of the engagement.

(b) **For small clients**: The responsibility for determining IR35 status rests with the Contractor (or the Contractor's intermediary).

(c) The Parties acknowledge their respective obligations under IR35 legislation and agree to cooperate in providing information necessary for any status determination.

### 5.3 Contractor's Obligations

(a) The Contractor warrants that the arrangements under this Agreement genuinely reflect a relationship of independent contracting and not employment.

(b) The Contractor shall notify the Client immediately if any factor changes that could affect the IR35 status determination.

### 5.4 Tax Indemnity

The Contractor shall indemnify and keep indemnified the Client against any liability for income tax, national insurance contributions, penalties, interest, and costs that HMRC may assess against the Client in respect of the Contractor's engagement, to the extent such liability arises from the Contractor's failure to discharge its own tax obligations or from a misrepresentation by the Contractor regarding its tax status.
