## Recitals

(A) The Controller and the Processor have entered into an agreement for the Processor to provide freelance marketplace services through the AllSquared platform (the "Services Agreement").

(B) In the course of providing services under the Services Agreement, the Processor processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.

(C) The Parties enter into this Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") to ensure that the processing of Personal Data complies with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR), the Data Protection Act 2018, and all applicable data protection legislation (together, "Data Protection Law").

(D) This DPA is supplemental to the Services Agreement and sets out the terms on which the Processor processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.

## 1. Definitions

1.1 In this DPA, the following terms have the meanings set out below. Where a term is defined in UK GDPR Article 4 and is not separately defined here, it has the meaning given to it in Article 4.

**"Controller"** has the meaning given in UK GDPR Article 4(7): the natural or legal person which, alone or jointly with others, determines the purposes and means of the processing of Personal Data.

**"Data Breach"** means a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data transmitted, stored, or otherwise processed.

**"Data Subject"** has the meaning given in UK GDPR Article 4(1): an identified or identifiable natural person to whom Personal Data relates.

**"Instructions"** means the documented instructions provided by the Controller to the Processor regarding the processing of Personal Data, as set out in Schedule 4 and as may be updated by the Controller from time to time in writing.

**"Personal Data"** has the meaning given in UK GDPR Article 4(1): any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.

**"Processing"** has the meaning given in UK GDPR Article 4(2): any operation or set of operations performed on Personal Data, whether or not by automated means.

**"Processor"** has the meaning given in UK GDPR Article 4(8): a natural or legal person which processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.

**"Security Incident"** means any event that may compromise the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of Personal Data processed under this DPA, whether or not it constitutes a Data Breach.

**"Sub-processor"** means any third party engaged by the Processor to process Personal Data on behalf of the Controller in connection with the services provided under the Services Agreement.

**"Supervisory Authority"** means the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) or any successor body.

**"Working Day"** means any day other than a Saturday, Sunday, or public holiday in England and Wales.

## 2. Scope of Processing

2.1 The Processor must process Personal Data only for the purposes of providing the services described in the Services Agreement and in accordance with the Controller's documented Instructions.

2.2 The details of the processing are set out in Schedule 1 (Processing Details), which forms part of this DPA and includes:

(a) **Subject matter:** Freelance marketplace services provided through the AllSquared platform, including project management, communication facilitation, and payment processing.

(b) **Duration:** The term of the Services Agreement plus any applicable retention periods as set out in the AllSquared Privacy Policy and this DPA.

(c) **Nature and purpose of processing:** Hosting, storing, organising, retrieving, and transmitting Personal Data to facilitate communications between the Controller's personnel and freelancers on the AllSquared platform; processing payment transactions; providing project management and escrow services; generating reports and analytics for the Controller.

(d) **Types of Personal Data:** Names, email addresses, telephone numbers, postal addresses, professional qualifications and experience, communications content (messages sent through the AllSquared platform), financial transaction data (payment amounts, bank account details, transaction references), and account credentials.

(e) **Categories of Data Subjects:** The Controller's employees, agents, and contractors who use the AllSquared platform; the Controller's clients or end-users whose Personal Data is processed through the platform in connection with projects managed via AllSquared.

## 3. Processor Obligations

### 3.1 Processing on Instructions

(a) The Processor must process Personal Data only on the Controller's documented Instructions, as set out in Schedule 4, unless required to do so by the laws of England and Wales or the United Kingdom. Where processing is required by law, the Processor must inform the Controller of that legal requirement before processing, unless the law prohibits such information on important grounds of public interest.

(b) The Processor must immediately inform the Controller if, in the Processor's opinion, an Instruction infringes Data Protection Law.

### 3.2 Confidentiality

(a) The Processor must ensure that all persons authorised to process Personal Data under this DPA are subject to binding written confidentiality obligations.

(b) The Processor must ensure that access to Personal Data is limited to those personnel who need access to perform the services under the Services Agreement.

### 3.3 Security Measures

(a) The Processor must implement and maintain appropriate technical and organisational security measures in accordance with UK GDPR Article 32, taking into account the state of the art, the costs of implementation, and the nature, scope, context, and purposes of processing, as well as the risk to the rights and freedoms of Data Subjects.

(b) The minimum security measures are set out in Schedule 3 (Security Measures) and include:

    (i) encryption of Personal Data in transit using TLS 1.2 or above;

    (ii) encryption of Personal Data at rest using AES-256 or equivalent;

    (iii) role-based access control limiting access to Personal Data on a need-to-know basis;

    (iv) multi-factor authentication (MFA) for all administrative and privileged access;

    (v) regular security testing, including penetration testing;

    (vi) pseudonymisation of Personal Data where appropriate and practicable.

(c) The Processor must regularly review and, where necessary, update the security measures to ensure ongoing effectiveness.

### 3.4 Sub-processors

(a) The Controller authorises the Processor to engage the Sub-processors listed in Schedule 2 (Sub-processors) as at the date of this DPA.

(b) The Processor must give the Controller at least 30 days' written notice before engaging any new Sub-processor or replacing an existing Sub-processor.

(c) The Controller may object to the appointment or replacement of a Sub-processor by giving the Processor written notice within 14 days of receiving notification under clause 3.4(b). The objection must set out reasonable grounds for the objection.

(d) If the Controller raises a valid objection, the Processor must not engage the proposed Sub-processor for the processing of the Controller's Personal Data. The Parties must cooperate in good faith to find an alternative solution.

(e) The Processor must enter into a written agreement with each Sub-processor that imposes data protection obligations no less protective than those set out in this DPA. The Processor remains fully liable to the Controller for the performance of each Sub-processor's obligations.

### 3.5 Data Subject Rights

(a) The Processor must assist the Controller in fulfilling its obligations to respond to Data Subject requests to exercise their rights under Chapter III of the UK GDPR (including rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and objection).

(b) The Processor must notify the Controller within 5 Working Days of receiving any request from a Data Subject in relation to Personal Data processed under this DPA. The Processor must not respond to a Data Subject request directly unless authorised in writing by the Controller.

(c) The Processor must provide reasonable assistance taking into account the nature of the processing and the information available to the Processor.

### 3.6 Data Breach Notification

(a) The Processor must notify the Controller without undue delay, and in any event within 48 hours, of becoming aware of any Data Breach affecting Personal Data processed under this DPA.

(b) The Processor must provide the Controller with a full written incident report within 72 hours of becoming aware of the Data Breach. The report must include:

    (i) the nature of the Data Breach, including the categories of Personal Data and Data Subjects concerned;

    (ii) the approximate number of Data Subjects affected;

    (iii) the likely consequences of the Data Breach;

    (iv) the measures taken or proposed to be taken to address the Data Breach, including measures to mitigate its possible adverse effects.

(c) The Processor must cooperate with the Controller and take all reasonable steps to assist in the investigation, mitigation, and remediation of the Data Breach.

(d) The Processor must document all Data Breaches, including the facts relating to the breach, its effects, and the remedial action taken.

### 3.7 Data Protection Impact Assessments

The Processor must provide reasonable assistance to the Controller in carrying out Data Protection Impact Assessments and prior consultations with the Supervisory Authority, where required under UK GDPR Articles 35 and 36, taking into account the nature of the processing and the information available to the Processor.

### 3.8 Deletion and Return of Data

(a) On termination or expiry of the Services Agreement, the Processor must, at the Controller's written election, either delete or return all Personal Data processed under this DPA within 30 days. The Processor must provide written confirmation of deletion.

(b) The Processor may retain Personal Data after termination only where retention is required by the laws of England and Wales or the United Kingdom. In such cases, the Processor must inform the Controller of the legal requirement, specify which data is retained and the reason for retention, and continue to protect the data in accordance with this DPA.

### 3.9 Audit Rights

(a) The Processor must make available to the Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with UK GDPR Article 28 and this DPA.

(b) The Controller may conduct an audit of the Processor's compliance with this DPA once per calendar year. The Controller must give at least 30 days' written notice of any audit. Audits must be conducted during normal business hours at the Controller's own cost.

(c) The Processor may satisfy its audit obligations under this clause 3.9 by providing the Controller with a current third-party audit report (such as SOC 2 Type II or equivalent) that covers the Processor's data processing activities. Where such a report is provided, the Controller is not entitled to conduct a separate on-site audit in respect of the matters covered by the report, unless the Controller has reasonable grounds to believe that the report is incomplete or inaccurate.

(d) The Processor must cooperate with the Controller and the Supervisory Authority in connection with any audit, inspection, or investigation.

## 4. Controller Obligations

4.1 The Controller must ensure that it has a valid lawful basis under UK GDPR Article 6 (and, where applicable, Article 9) for the processing of Personal Data that it instructs the Processor to carry out.

4.2 The Controller is responsible for responding to Data Subject requests, although the Controller may instruct the Processor to assist in accordance with clause 3.5.

4.3 The Controller must maintain its own Records of Processing Activities in accordance with UK GDPR Article 30.

4.4 The Controller must ensure that its Instructions to the Processor are lawful and comply with Data Protection Law. The Controller must not instruct the Processor to process Personal Data in a manner that would place the Processor in breach of Data Protection Law.

4.5 The Controller must notify the Processor of any restrictions or particular requirements relating to the processing of Personal Data that may affect the Processor's ability to perform its obligations under this DPA.

## 5. International Transfers

5.1 The Processor transfers Personal Data to the Sub-processors listed in Schedule 2 in the locations specified. Where a Sub-processor is located outside the United Kingdom, the Processor must ensure that the transfer is subject to appropriate safeguards in accordance with UK GDPR Chapter V.

5.2 The current transfer mechanisms for each Sub-processor are as follows:

(a) **Stripe Inc. (USA):** Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) adopted pursuant to the European Commission Decision 2021/914, as supplemented by the International Data Transfer Addendum to the EU Commission Standard Contractual Clauses (the "UK Addendum") issued by the ICO under Section 119A of the Data Protection Act 2018.

(b) **Clerk Inc. (USA):** SCCs with UK Addendum.

(c) **Neon Inc. (EU — Frankfurt):** UK-EU adequacy decision or, where applicable, SCCs with UK Addendum.

(d) **Vercel Inc. (USA/EU):** SCCs with UK Addendum.

5.3 The Processor must ensure that any international transfer of Personal Data provides an equivalent level of protection to that guaranteed by Data Protection Law.

5.4 The Processor must provide the Controller with details of the transfer mechanisms relied upon on request.

5.5 If a transfer mechanism relied upon by a Sub-processor is invalidated or otherwise ceases to provide adequate protection, the Processor must promptly notify the Controller and take reasonable steps to implement an alternative lawful transfer mechanism.

## 6. Liability

6.1 Each Party is liable for administrative fines imposed by the Supervisory Authority to the extent that those fines are attributable to that Party's own breach of Data Protection Law.

6.2 The Processor's general liability to the Controller arising under or in connection with this DPA is governed by the liability provisions of the Services Agreement. No separate liability cap applies under this DPA unless the Parties agree otherwise in writing.

6.3 The Controller and the Processor must cooperate in good faith in the event of any regulatory investigation, complaint, or claim by a Data Subject relating to Personal Data processed under this DPA.

6.4 Neither Party excludes or limits its liability for fraud, for death or personal injury caused by its negligence, or for any other liability that cannot be excluded or limited by law.

## 7. Term and Termination

7.1 This DPA commences on the Effective Date and continues in force for the duration of the Services Agreement. This DPA automatically terminates on termination or expiry of the Services Agreement, subject to clause 7.2.

7.2 The data protection obligations in this DPA survive termination or expiry of the Services Agreement for the duration of any applicable retention period and until all Personal Data has been deleted or returned in accordance with clause 3.8.

7.3 On termination, the Processor must comply with the deletion and return obligations set out in clause 3.8 within 30 days.

## 8. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

8.1 This DPA is governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of England and Wales.

8.2 The courts of England and Wales have exclusive jurisdiction in relation to any dispute arising out of or in connection with this DPA.

8.3 The Supervisory Authority for the purposes of this DPA is the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO).

## 9. General

9.1 This DPA (including its Schedules) constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties in relation to the processing of Personal Data under the Services Agreement and supersedes all prior agreements, representations, and understandings relating to data processing.

9.2 No variation of this DPA is effective unless made in writing and signed by both Parties.

9.3 If any provision of this DPA is found to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions continue in full force and effect.

9.4 In the event of any conflict between this DPA and the Services Agreement, this DPA prevails to the extent that the conflict relates to data protection matters.

## Schedule 3 — Security Measures

The Processor implements and maintains the following technical and organisational security measures:

**Encryption**

| Measure | Standard |
|---|---|
| Data in transit | TLS 1.2 or above for all connections |
| Data at rest | AES-256 encryption for all stored Personal Data |

**Access Control**

| Measure | Detail |
|---|---|
| Role-based access control (RBAC) | Access to Personal Data limited on a need-to-know basis by role assignment |
| Multi-factor authentication (MFA) | Mandatory for all administrative and privileged access to systems containing Personal Data |
| Principle of least privilege | User accounts provisioned with the minimum access necessary for role function |

**Testing and Monitoring**

| Measure | Frequency / Detail |
|---|---|
| Penetration testing | Quarterly, conducted by qualified third-party security assessors |
| Vulnerability scanning | Continuous automated scanning of production infrastructure |
| Access logging and monitoring | All access to systems containing Personal Data logged and monitored; logs retained for a minimum of 12 months |
| SOC 2 Type II | Target certification (or equivalent third-party audit) |

**Incident Response**

| Measure | Detail |
|---|---|
| Incident response plan | Documented and tested incident response plan covering detection, containment, eradication, recovery, and post-incident review |
| Business continuity and disaster recovery | Documented BCP/DR plan with defined recovery time objectives (RTO) and recovery point objectives (RPO) |

**Personnel**

| Measure | Frequency / Detail |
|---|---|
| Employee security training | Annual mandatory training for all personnel with access to Personal Data |
| Confidentiality agreements | Binding written confidentiality obligations for all personnel |
| Background checks | Conducted for personnel with access to sensitive systems, where permitted by law |

**Vendor Management**

| Measure | Detail |
|---|---|
| Vendor security assessments | Security assessments conducted for all Sub-processors before onboarding and reviewed annually |
| Contractual protections | Written data processing agreements with all Sub-processors imposing equivalent security obligations |

**Data Management**

| Measure | Detail |
|---|---|
| Pseudonymisation | Applied where appropriate and practicable to reduce the identifiability of Data Subjects |
| Data minimisation | Only Personal Data necessary for the stated purpose is collected and processed |
| Secure deletion | Personal Data securely deleted in accordance with documented retention schedules using industry-standard methods |
