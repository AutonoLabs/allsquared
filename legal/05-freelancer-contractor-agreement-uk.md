## Background

(a) The Client wishes to engage the Freelancer to provide the services described in Schedule 1 as an independent contractor.

(b) The Freelancer has the skills and experience to provide those services and wishes to do so on the terms set out in this Agreement.

(c) The Parties have agreed to use AllSquared (allsquared.io) ("AllSquared" or the "Platform") as the platform through which the engagement is managed and payments are processed.

## 1. Definitions and Interpretation

1.1 In this Agreement, unless the context requires otherwise, the following terms have the meanings set out below:

(a) "Confidential Information" means all information (whether disclosed in writing, orally, electronically, or by any other means) that is designated as confidential or that a reasonable person would consider confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure, including business plans, financial data, technical specifications, client lists, pricing, trade secrets, and the terms of this Agreement.

(b) "Deliverables" means all tangible and intangible outputs, work product, reports, documents, designs, software, and other materials to be produced by the Freelancer in the course of performing the Services, as described in Schedule 1.

(c) "Engagement Period" means the period from {{startDate}} to {{endDate}}, unless terminated earlier or extended in accordance with this Agreement.

(d) "Expenses Policy" means the expenses policy set out in Schedule 2.

(e) "Fee" means the daily rate of {{dailyRate}} or the fixed fee of {{fixedFee}}, as applicable and as further specified in Schedule 1.

(f) "IP Rights" means all intellectual property rights of any kind, including patents, trade marks, rights in designs, copyright, database rights, rights in know-how, trade secrets, and all other intellectual property rights, in each case whether registered or unregistered and including all applications and rights to apply for and be granted renewals or extensions of, and rights to claim priority from, such rights and all similar or equivalent rights or forms of protection in any part of the world.

(g) "Services" means the services to be provided by the Freelancer to the Client as described in Schedule 1.

(h) "Working Day" means any day other than a Saturday, Sunday, or public holiday in England and Wales.

1.2 In this Agreement:

(a) a reference to a statute or statutory provision includes any subordinate legislation made under it and any modification, re-enactment, or replacement of it from time to time;

(b) a reference to writing or written includes email;

(c) the words "include", "including", and "in particular" do not limit the generality of any preceding words; and

(d) headings are for convenience only and do not affect interpretation.

## 2. Engagement

2.1 The Client engages the Freelancer, and the Freelancer accepts the engagement, to provide the Services on the terms of this Agreement. The engagement is that of an independent contractor, and this Agreement does not create a contract of employment, partnership, joint venture, or agency.

2.2 Payment processing, escrow, and related administrative functions for this engagement are governed by AllSquared's terms of service. Both Parties must maintain active accounts on the Platform for the duration of the Engagement Period.

## 3. Services

3.1 The Freelancer must provide the Services described in Schedule 1 with reasonable skill, care, and diligence and in accordance with good industry practice.

3.2 The Freelancer is responsible for determining the manner, method, and means by which the Services are performed, subject to meeting the requirements specified in Schedule 1 and any reasonable directions from the Client regarding the desired outcomes.

3.3 The Freelancer must not subcontract or delegate the performance of any part of the Services to any third party without the prior written consent of the Client. Any approved subcontractor must:

(a) be subject to confidentiality obligations no less onerous than those set out in clause 7; and

(b) enter into an agreement with the Freelancer that assigns all IP Rights in work product to the Freelancer (for onward assignment to the Client under clause 6).

3.4 The Freelancer must comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and professional standards in performing the Services.

## 4. Term

4.1 This Agreement commences on {{startDate}} and continues until {{endDate}} ("Engagement Period"), unless terminated earlier in accordance with clause 12.

4.2 The Parties may extend the Engagement Period by written agreement signed by both Parties.

4.3 Either Party may terminate this Agreement by giving {{noticePeriod}} written notice to the other Party, subject to clause 12.

## 5. Fees and Expenses

5.1 The Client must pay the Freelancer the Fee as specified in Schedule 1, at the daily rate of {{dailyRate}} or the fixed fee of {{fixedFee}}, as applicable, in accordance with the payment terms of {{paymentTerms}}.

5.2 Where the Fee is charged on a daily rate basis, the Freelancer must submit itemised invoices to the Client monthly in arrears, detailing the days worked and the Services performed. Where the Fee is a fixed fee, invoices must be submitted on completion of the relevant milestones specified in Schedule 1.

5.3 All payments must be processed through AllSquared's escrow facility. The AllSquared platform fee of 10% is deducted from the Freelancer's payment before release. The Freelancer acknowledges and accepts this deduction.

5.4 All fees are exclusive of VAT. If VAT is chargeable on any supply made by the Freelancer, the Client must pay the VAT amount in addition to the Fee, subject to receipt of a valid VAT invoice.

5.5 The Client must reimburse the Freelancer for expenses incurred in the performance of the Services only in accordance with the Expenses Policy in Schedule 2 and only where such expenses have been pre-approved in writing by the Client.

5.6 If the Client fails to make any undisputed payment when due, interest accrues on the overdue amount at the rate specified in the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998, calculated on a daily basis from the due date until actual payment (whether before or after judgment).

## 6. Intellectual Property

6.1 All IP Rights in the Deliverables created by the Freelancer in the performance of the Services vest in the Freelancer upon creation and are hereby assigned to the Client with full title guarantee, effective upon receipt of full payment of the Fee for the relevant Deliverables. The Freelancer must execute all documents and do all acts reasonably required to perfect the assignment.

6.2 The Freelancer warrants that the Freelancer is the sole owner of (or has the right to assign) all IP Rights in the Deliverables and that the Deliverables do not infringe the IP Rights of any third party.

6.3 To the extent that any pre-existing intellectual property of the Freelancer ("Background IP") is incorporated into the Deliverables, the Freelancer grants the Client a non-exclusive, perpetual, irrevocable, royalty-free, worldwide licence to use, modify, reproduce, and sublicence that Background IP solely as part of or in connection with the Deliverables.

6.4 To the fullest extent permitted by law, the Freelancer irrevocably and unconditionally waives all moral rights under Chapter IV of Part I of the Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988 in respect of the Deliverables.

## 7. Confidentiality

7.1 Each Party ("Receiving Party") must keep confidential all Confidential Information received from the other Party ("Disclosing Party") and must not disclose it to any third party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party.

7.2 The obligations in clause 7.1 do not apply to information that:

(a) is or becomes publicly available other than through a breach of this Agreement;

(b) was already known to the Receiving Party before disclosure, as evidenced by written records;

(c) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without reference to the Confidential Information; or

(d) is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or order of a court or competent authority, provided the Receiving Party gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice (to the extent legally permitted) and cooperates with any steps to limit the scope of such disclosure.

7.3 Each Party must use the other Party's Confidential Information only for the purposes of this Agreement.

7.4 Upon termination or expiry of this Agreement, each Party must promptly return or destroy (at the Disclosing Party's election) all Confidential Information of the other Party, except to the extent that retention is required by applicable law or regulation.

7.5 The obligations under this clause 7 survive termination or expiry of this Agreement for a period of 5 years.

## 8. Data Protection

8.1 Each Party must comply with all applicable data protection legislation, including the UK General Data Protection Regulation and the Data Protection Act 2018 ("UK GDPR").

8.2 If the Freelancer processes personal data on behalf of the Client in the course of performing the Services, the Parties must execute a data processing agreement that complies with Article 28 of the UK GDPR, under which:

(a) the Client is the data controller and the Freelancer is the data processor;

(b) the Freelancer must process personal data only on the Client's documented instructions;

(c) the Freelancer must implement appropriate technical and organisational security measures; and

(d) the Freelancer must delete or return all personal data to the Client on termination of this Agreement.

8.3 The Freelancer must not transfer personal data outside the United Kingdom without the Client's prior written consent and without ensuring adequate safeguards in accordance with the UK GDPR.

## 9. Warranties

9.1 The Freelancer warrants and represents that:

(a) the Freelancer has the necessary skills, qualifications, and experience to perform the Services to a professional standard;

(b) the Freelancer will perform the Services in compliance with all applicable laws and regulations;

(c) the Freelancer has no conflict of interest that would materially affect the performance of the Services and must promptly disclose any such conflict that arises during the Engagement Period; and

(d) the Freelancer has full authority and capacity to enter into this Agreement.

9.2 The Client warrants and represents that:

(a) the Client will provide the Freelancer with timely access to information, personnel, systems, and resources reasonably required for the performance of the Services;

(b) the Client will pay the Fees when due in accordance with this Agreement; and

(c) the Client has full authority and capacity to enter into this Agreement.

## 10. Independent Contractor Status

10.1 The Freelancer is engaged as an independent contractor. Nothing in this Agreement creates a relationship of employment between the Freelancer (or any of the Freelancer's personnel) and the Client.

10.2 The Freelancer is solely responsible for:

(a) all income tax (including under the Pay As You Earn system), National Insurance contributions, and any VAT arising from the fees received under this Agreement;

(b) registering for self-assessment with HM Revenue & Customs and making all required returns and payments; and

(c) any pension, holiday pay, sick pay, parental leave, or other employment-related benefits, none of which are provided by the Client.

10.3 The Freelancer must indemnify the Client against any claim by HM Revenue & Customs or any other tax authority for income tax, National Insurance contributions, or other amounts that should have been deducted from the Freelancer's fees.

10.4 The Freelancer is responsible for assessing and managing compliance with the off-payroll working rules (commonly known as "IR35") under Chapter 10 of Part 2 of the Income Tax (Earnings and Pensions) Act 2003 (as amended). The Freelancer must notify the Client promptly if the Freelancer forms the view that the engagement falls within the off-payroll working rules and must cooperate with any status determination made by the Client.

## 11. Restrictions

11.1 During the Engagement Period, the Freelancer must not provide services to a direct competitor of the Client (being a business that offers substantially similar products or services in the same market) without first disclosing that engagement to the Client in writing and obtaining the Client's written consent, such consent not to be unreasonably withheld.

11.2 For a period of 6 months following the end of the Engagement Period, the Freelancer must not, directly or indirectly:

(a) solicit or entice away (or attempt to solicit or entice away) any client or customer of the Client with whom the Freelancer had material dealings during the Engagement Period; or

(b) solicit or recruit (or attempt to solicit or recruit) any employee or contractor of the Client with whom the Freelancer worked during the Engagement Period.

11.3 The restrictions in clause 11.2 are considered by the Parties to be reasonable in scope and duration. If any restriction is held to be unenforceable, it must be deemed modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it enforceable.

## 12. Limitation of Liability

12.1 Nothing in this Agreement limits or excludes either Party's liability for:

(a) death or personal injury caused by its negligence;

(b) fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation; or

(c) any other liability that cannot be limited or excluded by applicable law.

12.2 Subject to clause 12.1, neither Party is liable to the other for any indirect, consequential, special, or incidental loss or damage, including loss of profit, loss of revenue, loss of business, loss of data, or loss of goodwill.

12.3 Subject to clause 12.1, the Freelancer's total aggregate liability under or in connection with this Agreement must not exceed the total Fees paid to the Freelancer in the 12-month period immediately preceding the event giving rise to the claim.

12.4 Subject to clause 12.1, the Client's total aggregate liability under or in connection with this Agreement must not exceed the total Fees payable by the Client under this Agreement.

## 13. Indemnity

13.1 The Freelancer must indemnify and hold harmless the Client against all losses, damages, costs, and expenses (including reasonable legal fees) arising from:

(a) any claim that the Deliverables infringe the IP Rights of a third party, except to the extent arising from the Client's materials or instructions;

(b) any breach by the Freelancer of its obligations under clause 8 (Data Protection); or

(c) the Freelancer's wilful misconduct or gross negligence.

13.2 The Client must indemnify and hold harmless the Freelancer against all losses, damages, costs, and expenses (including reasonable legal fees) arising from:

(a) any claim that the Client's materials or Background IP provided to the Freelancer infringe the IP Rights of a third party;

(b) any breach by the Client of its obligations under clause 8 (Data Protection); or

(c) the Client's wilful misconduct or gross negligence.

## 14. Termination

14.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement for cause by giving written notice if:

(a) the other Party commits a material breach of this Agreement and fails to remedy that breach within 14 days of receiving written notice specifying the breach and requiring its remedy; or

(b) the other Party becomes insolvent, enters administration or liquidation, or makes any arrangement with its creditors.

14.2 Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience by giving {{noticePeriod}} written notice to the other Party.

14.3 Upon termination or expiry for any reason:

(a) the Freelancer must deliver to the Client all completed and partially completed Deliverables;

(b) the Client must pay for all Services performed and Deliverables accepted up to the date of termination, and Escrow funds for completed work must be released to the Freelancer;

(c) any Escrow funds held for work not yet completed or accepted must be returned to the Client unless the Parties agree otherwise;

(d) each Party must return or destroy all Confidential Information of the other Party; and

(e) clauses 6, 7, 8, 10, 11, 12, 13, and 16 survive termination.

## 15. Dispute Resolution

15.1 The Parties must attempt to resolve any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement through good-faith negotiation within 30 days of written notice of the dispute.

15.2 If the dispute is not resolved within the period specified in clause 15.1, either Party may refer the dispute to arbitration under the rules of the London Court of International Arbitration ("LCIA"). The arbitration must be conducted in London by a single arbitrator appointed in accordance with the LCIA Rules. The language of the arbitration must be English.

15.3 Nothing in this clause 15 prevents either Party from applying to the courts of England and Wales for interim or injunctive relief.

## 16. General

16.1 **Entire agreement.** This Agreement (including all Schedules) constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties in relation to its subject matter and supersedes all previous agreements, understandings, and representations.

16.2 **Variation.** No variation of this Agreement is effective unless it is in writing and signed by or on behalf of both Parties.

16.3 **Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is held by a court or competent authority to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, that provision must be deemed modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid, legal, and enforceable. If modification is not possible, the provision must be deemed deleted without affecting the remaining provisions.

16.4 **Waiver.** A waiver of any right or remedy is only effective if given in writing and is not to be taken as a waiver of any subsequent right or remedy. A failure or delay by a Party to exercise any right does not constitute a waiver.

16.5 **Assignment.** The Freelancer must not assign, transfer, or subcontract any rights or obligations under this Agreement without the Client's prior written consent. The Client may assign its rights to a successor in title to all or substantially all of its business.

16.6 **Notices.** Any notice must be in writing and delivered by email to the addresses specified in this Agreement. A notice is deemed received on the Working Day after it is sent.

16.7 **Force majeure.** Neither Party is liable for any failure or delay in performing its obligations to the extent caused by circumstances beyond its reasonable control, including acts of God, natural disaster, pandemic, war, terrorism, riot, government action, fire, flood, or industrial action not involving the affected Party's employees. The affected Party must notify the other promptly and use reasonable endeavours to mitigate the effect. If the event continues for more than 60 days, either Party may terminate by written notice.

16.8 **Third-party rights.** No person other than the Parties has any rights under the Contracts (Rights of Third Parties) Act 1999 to enforce any term of this Agreement.

## 17. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

17.1 This Agreement and any dispute or claim arising out of or in connection with it or its subject matter or formation (including non-contractual disputes or claims) is governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of England and Wales.

17.2 Subject to clause 15, the courts of England and Wales have exclusive jurisdiction to settle any dispute or claim arising out of or in connection with this Agreement.

## Schedule 2 — Expenses Policy

### 1. General Principles

(a) The Freelancer may claim reimbursement only for expenses that are reasonable, necessary for the performance of the Services, and incurred in accordance with this policy.

(b) All expenses exceeding the pre-approval threshold must be approved in writing by the Client before they are incurred.

(c) The Client is not obliged to reimburse any expense that does not comply with this policy.

### 2. Allowable Expense Categories

| Category | Description | Pre-Approval Threshold |
|----------|-------------|----------------------|
| Travel | Train, bus, taxi, economy-class flights directly related to the Services | Exceeding £100 per journey |
| Accommodation | Hotel or serviced apartment for overnight stays required by the engagement | All accommodation |
| Subsistence | Reasonable meals and refreshments when working away from the Freelancer's normal base | Exceeding £30 per day |
| Equipment and Software | Equipment or software licences specifically required for the Services and not already available | All items |
| Printing and Postage | Printing, copying, and postage directly related to Deliverables | Exceeding £25 per item |
| Other | Any other expense directly and necessarily incurred for the Services | All items |

### 3. Receipts and Evidence

(a) The Freelancer must retain and submit original receipts or electronic copies for all expenses claimed.

(b) Each expense claim must include the date, description, amount, category, and business justification.

### 4. Submission and Payment

(a) The Freelancer must submit expense claims within 30 days of the expense being incurred.

(b) Expense claims must be submitted through the AllSquared Platform or by email to the Client with supporting receipts.

(c) The Client must review and approve or reject expense claims within 10 Working Days of receipt.

(d) Approved expenses are paid through AllSquared Escrow together with the next Fee payment or within 14 days of approval, whichever is sooner.

### 5. Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following are not reimbursable:

(a) fines, penalties, or parking tickets;

(b) personal entertainment or gifts;

(c) expenses related to the Freelancer's general business overheads (office rent, internet, phone, general equipment);

(d) first-class or business-class travel (unless pre-approved in writing); and

(e) any expense not directly related to the performance of the Services.
