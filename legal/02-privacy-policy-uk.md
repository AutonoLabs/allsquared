# AllSquared Ltd — Privacy Policy

**Last updated:** March 2025

**Data Controller:** AllSquared Ltd, a company registered in England and Wales

**Contact:** legal@allsquared.io

**Data Protection Officer:** dpo@allsquared.io

**Website:** allsquared.io (the "Platform")

## 1. Introduction

1.1 AllSquared Ltd ("AllSquared", "we", "us", or "our") operates the AllSquared platform at allsquared.io, a freelance services marketplace connecting clients with freelancers for service contracts with milestone and escrow payments.

1.2 This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, share, and protect your personal data when you use the Platform. It applies to all users of the Platform, including visitors, registered users, clients, and freelancers.

1.3 AllSquared Ltd is the data controller for the personal data processed through the Platform. We are registered in England and Wales.

1.4 We have appointed a Data Protection Officer who can be contacted at dpo@allsquared.io for any queries relating to this Privacy Policy or your data protection rights.

1.5 We process personal data in accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) as retained under the European Union (Withdrawal) Act 2018, the Data Protection Act 2018, and the Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations 2003.

## 2. Information We Collect

2.1 We collect and process the following categories of personal data:

**Identity Data**

2.2 This includes your first name, last name, username, date of birth, and any identification documents you provide for verification purposes. We collect Identity Data when you register for an account, complete identity verification, or update your profile.

**Contact Data**

2.3 This includes your email address, telephone number, and postal address. We collect Contact Data when you register for an account, contact us, or update your account information.

**Financial Data**

2.4 This includes your bank account details, payment card details (processed and stored by Stripe, not stored directly by AllSquared), and billing address. We collect Financial Data when you add a payment method, receive a payout, or fund an escrow deposit.

**Transactional Data**

2.5 This includes details of payments made to and from your account, escrow deposits and releases, service fees deducted, and the history of Service Contracts and Milestones associated with your account.

**Technical Data**

2.6 This includes your IP address, browser type and version, operating system, device identifiers, time zone setting, and other technical information collected automatically when you access the Platform. We also collect information about your internet connection and the equipment you use to access the Platform.

**Usage Data**

2.7 This includes information about how you use the Platform, including pages visited, features used, search queries, click patterns, time spent on pages, and navigation paths. We use this data to understand how users interact with the Platform and to improve our services.

**Communications Data**

2.8 This includes the content of messages exchanged between users through the Platform's messaging system, communications between you and AllSquared (including support tickets and emails), and any reviews or feedback you submit through the Platform.

**Profile Data**

2.9 This includes your profile information, portfolio items, skills, qualifications, work history, ratings, reviews, and any other information you choose to include in your public profile on the Platform.

## 3. Lawful Basis for Processing

3.1 We process your personal data on the following lawful bases under Article 6 of the UK GDPR:

**Performance of Contract**

3.2 We process Identity Data, Contact Data, Financial Data, Transactional Data, Communications Data, and Profile Data where necessary for the performance of our contract with you (the Terms of Service), including:

(a) creating and managing your account;

(b) facilitating Service Contracts between Clients and Freelancers;

(c) operating the escrow system, processing payments, and issuing payouts;

(d) providing customer support; and

(e) resolving Disputes between users.

**Legal Obligation**

3.3 We process Identity Data, Contact Data, Financial Data, and Transactional Data where necessary to comply with our legal obligations, including:

(a) anti-money laundering and counter-terrorism financing requirements;

(b) tax reporting obligations;

(c) fraud prevention and detection; and

(d) responding to lawful requests from law enforcement and regulatory authorities.

**Legitimate Interests**

3.4 We process Technical Data, Usage Data, and Communications Data where necessary for our legitimate interests, provided those interests are not overridden by your rights and freedoms. Our legitimate interests include:

(a) maintaining the security and integrity of the Platform;

(b) analysing usage patterns to improve the Platform's functionality and user experience;

(c) detecting and preventing fraud, abuse, and violations of our Terms of Service;

(d) enforcing our Terms of Service; and

(e) internal record-keeping and administrative purposes.

**Consent**

3.5 We process your data on the basis of consent where we send you marketing communications, newsletters, or promotional materials. You may withdraw your consent at any time by using the unsubscribe link in any marketing email or by contacting us at legal@allsquared.io. Withdrawal of consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before the withdrawal.

## 4. How We Use Your Data

4.1 We use your personal data for the following purposes:

(a) **Account management.** Creating, maintaining, and administering your account, verifying your identity, and managing your profile.

(b) **Service delivery.** Facilitating Service Contracts, enabling communication between Clients and Freelancers, displaying profiles and portfolios, and managing the Milestone workflow.

(c) **Payment processing.** Processing escrow deposits, releasing payouts to Freelancers, deducting AllSquared service fees, generating invoices and fee statements, and managing payment methods via Stripe.

(d) **Fraud prevention.** Detecting, investigating, and preventing fraudulent activity, unauthorised access, and other illegal or harmful conduct on the Platform.

(e) **Platform improvement.** Analysing usage data and user feedback to improve the Platform's features, functionality, performance, and user experience. This includes A/B testing and analytics.

(f) **Marketing.** Sending you marketing communications, newsletters, and promotional offers where you have provided your consent. We do not sell your personal data to third-party marketers.

(g) **Legal compliance.** Complying with applicable laws, regulations, and legal processes, including anti-money laundering obligations, tax reporting, and responding to lawful requests from public authorities.

(h) **Dispute resolution.** Facilitating the resolution of Disputes between users, conducting mediation, and providing information required for arbitration or court proceedings.

## 5. Data Sharing

5.1 We do not sell your personal data to third parties. We share your personal data only in the following circumstances and with the following recipients:

**Service Providers**

5.2 We share data with the following third-party service providers who process data on our behalf:

(a) **Stripe** (Stripe, Inc., United States) — payment processing. Stripe processes your Financial Data and Transactional Data to facilitate payments, escrow deposits, and payouts. Data transfers to the United States are protected by Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the UK Information Commissioner's Office. Stripe's privacy policy is available at https://stripe.com/privacy.

(b) **Clerk** (Clerk, Inc., United States) — authentication and user management. Clerk processes your Identity Data and Contact Data to manage user authentication, session management, and account security. Data transfers to the United States are protected by Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs). Clerk's privacy policy is available at https://clerk.com/privacy.

(c) **Neon** (Neon, Inc., European Union) — database hosting. Neon hosts our database infrastructure within the European Union and processes data in accordance with the UK GDPR. Neon's privacy policy is available at https://neon.tech/privacy.

**Other Users**

5.3 When you use the Platform, certain information is shared with other users as necessary for the operation of the marketplace. For example, your profile information is visible to other users, and your communications within a Service Contract are visible to the other party to that contract.

**Legal and Regulatory Authorities**

5.4 We may disclose your personal data to law enforcement agencies, courts, regulatory authorities, and other public bodies where we are required to do so by law, regulation, or court order, or where disclosure is necessary for the prevention or detection of crime or fraud.

**Business Transfers**

5.5 If AllSquared is involved in a merger, acquisition, reorganisation, sale of assets, or insolvency, your personal data may be transferred to the successor entity as part of that transaction. We will notify you of any such transfer and any changes to the processing of your data.

**Professional Advisers**

5.6 We may share your personal data with our professional advisers, including lawyers, accountants, auditors, and insurers, where necessary for the provision of professional services to AllSquared.

## 6. Data Retention

6.1 We retain your personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, including to satisfy any legal, accounting, or reporting requirements.

6.2 The following retention periods apply:

(a) **Account data** (Identity Data, Contact Data, Profile Data): retained for the duration of your account and for 7 years after account closure, to comply with legal and regulatory requirements.

(b) **Transaction data** (Financial Data, Transactional Data): retained for 7 years from the date of the transaction, in accordance with HMRC record-keeping requirements and anti-money laundering regulations.

(c) **Communications data** (messages, support tickets, reviews): retained for 3 years from the date of the communication, or longer where required for the resolution of an active Dispute or legal proceedings.

(d) **Technical logs** (Technical Data, Usage Data): retained for 12 months from the date of collection, after which they are anonymised or deleted.

(e) **Marketing preferences**: retained until you withdraw your consent, after which they are deleted promptly.

6.3 At the end of the applicable retention period, personal data is securely deleted or anonymised so that it can no longer be associated with you.

## 7. Your Rights

7.1 Under the UK GDPR, you have the following rights in relation to your personal data:

(a) **Right of access.** You have the right to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you. This is commonly known as a subject access request (SAR).

(b) **Right to rectification.** You have the right to request that we correct any inaccurate or incomplete personal data we hold about you.

(c) **Right to erasure.** You have the right to request that we delete your personal data in certain circumstances, including where the data is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, where you withdraw consent, or where the data has been unlawfully processed. This right is not absolute and may be subject to legal obligations requiring us to retain data.

(d) **Right to restriction of processing.** You have the right to request that we restrict the processing of your personal data in certain circumstances, including where you contest the accuracy of the data or where you object to processing based on legitimate interests.

(e) **Right to data portability.** You have the right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format and to transmit that data to another data controller, where the processing is based on consent or performance of a contract and is carried out by automated means.

(f) **Right to object.** You have the right to object to the processing of your personal data where the processing is based on legitimate interests. We will cease processing unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds for the processing that override your interests, rights, and freedoms.

(g) **Rights relating to automated decision-making.** You have the right not to be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing, including profiling, that produces legal effects or similarly significant effects on you. AllSquared does not currently make decisions based solely on automated processing that produce legal or similarly significant effects.

7.2 To exercise any of your rights, please contact us at dpo@allsquared.io. We will respond to your request within 30 calendar days. In complex cases or where we receive a high volume of requests, we may extend this period by a further 60 calendar days, in which case we will inform you of the extension and the reasons for it.

7.3 We do not charge a fee for exercising your rights, except where a request is manifestly unfounded or excessive, in which case we may charge a reasonable fee or refuse to act on the request.

7.4 We may ask you to verify your identity before processing your request to protect the security of your personal data.

## 10. Security

10.1 We take appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised or unlawful processing, accidental loss, destruction, or damage. These measures include:

(a) **Encryption in transit.** All data transmitted between your browser and the Platform is encrypted using Transport Layer Security (TLS) 1.2 or higher.

(b) **Encryption at rest.** Personal data stored in our databases is encrypted at rest using industry-standard encryption algorithms.

(c) **Access controls.** Access to personal data is restricted to authorised personnel who need access to perform their duties. Access is controlled through role-based access controls, multi-factor authentication, and regular access reviews.

(d) **Regular testing.** We conduct regular security assessments and vulnerability testing of the Platform and our infrastructure.

(e) **Incident response.** We maintain an incident response plan to detect, respond to, and recover from data security incidents. In the event of a personal data breach that is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the Information Commissioner's Office within 72 hours and will notify you without undue delay where the breach is likely to result in a high risk to your rights and freedoms.

10.2 While we take all reasonable steps to protect your personal data, no method of transmission over the internet or method of electronic storage is completely secure. We cannot guarantee the absolute security of your data.
